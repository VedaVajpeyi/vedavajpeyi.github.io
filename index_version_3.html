About

I'm Veda Vajpeyi — a product leader and strategist who started by studying people long before I ever studied product.

My background is in sociology. That's not a detour from where I ended up; it's the foundation of how I work. I think about the psychology behind decisions, not just the data in front of me. When I'm building something, I'm asking: what are people actually trying to feel? Where does friction live that they can't see but I can? What does clarity look like to a person who never asked to be a user?

At Amazon, I work on Alexa — designing voice experiences that reach people in the small, unguarded moments of their day. The kitchen. The car. The early morning. I care about building things that earn trust without demanding attention.

I'm also a founder. I built No Desk Project because I believed location-independent work deserved more than just a laptop and a wifi signal — it deserved community, intention, and a real reason to show up. I lead Nomadize with the same conviction.

I hold an MBA from the University of Chicago Booth School of Business. I build with heart and rigor. I make complex things clear. And I care, genuinely, about the human on the other end — not as a persona in a deck, but as the whole, specific, complicated person they actually are.

Now

Shipping voice products at Amazon. Building Nomadize. Thinking about what makes some products feel effortless and others feel like work. Occasionally writing about it.
