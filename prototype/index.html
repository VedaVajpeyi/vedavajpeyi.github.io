01 · About

I trained as a
sociologist.

My work is in product strategy. The thing that connects them: most organizational and product problems are really behavioral problems in disguise. I start with the human logic underneath every system — what people are optimizing for, what they're afraid of, and what they need to feel before they will act.

At Amazon, I worked on Alexa for years — from individual PM to Chief of Staff to the VP of Alexa Devices and Software. Responsible for roadmap, monetization strategy, and cross-org initiatives at scale.

Before that I founded No Desk Project: a platform for location-independent professionals that reached 2,000+ members across 6 countries and was accepted into the GSVlabs Pioneer Accelerator. I hold an MBA from Chicago Booth and a BA in Sociology from UCLA.

Also: designed and taught a full-credit leadership course to 120 first-year MBA students — and found that they taught me far more than I taught them. Currently building Books & Beds, a boutique hotel in Bangalore. And genuinely convinced that the messier the problem, the more interesting the work.

03 · Active Project
↗ Books & Beds
In Development · Bangalore, India

Books & Beds

A boutique hotel built around a single conviction: that a room full of books changes how a person feels about where they are.

It's also a live experiment in what product thinking looks like when the medium is physical space — where trust, atmosphere, and experience can't be patched in a sprint. Floor-to-ceiling arched bookshelves. Rolling library ladders. A place designed to slow you down.

Currently in development.
