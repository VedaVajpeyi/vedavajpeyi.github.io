Books & Beds

Boutique Hotel · Bangalore, India · In Development

A boutique hotel for the traveler who wants to sleep surrounded by books. Floor-to-ceiling arched bookshelves, rolling library ladders, and the particular quiet of a room that takes reading seriously.

The Idea

Books & Beds started with a simple observation: the best hotels are the ones that have a point of view. Not a concept, not a theme — a genuine point of view about what makes a place worth being in.

For me, that point of view is books. Not as decoration. As the organizing principle of the space: the reason you linger, the reason you talk to the stranger at the next table, the reason the morning feels different than it would at any other hotel.

Bangalore is a city that reads. It has a literary culture, a tech culture, a café culture — and no hotel that truly belongs to all three. Books & Beds is designed to be that hotel.

What It Is

A small, intentional property. Floor-to-ceiling arched bookshelves lining the common areas and guest rooms. Rolling library ladders. A curated collection organized not by genre, but by the kind of reader you are on a given day — the kind who wants to be challenged, the kind who wants to disappear, the kind who wants to find something they didn't know they were looking for.

Every room has its own shelf. Every shelf has been chosen. Nothing is there for decoration.

The hospitality model is simple: make the kind of place you would genuinely want to stay in, and trust that other people exist who feel the same way.

Why This, Why Now

The question I get most often is: why hospitality? You're a product person.

The honest answer is that Books & Beds is a product problem. The unit of analysis is a guest, not a user. The surface is physical, not digital. But the underlying questions are identical: What does this person actually need to feel? What friction are they carrying in that they haven't named? What would make this experience feel, quietly, like it was designed for them specifically?

The question that started No Desk Project — what helps someone feel grounded where they are — is the same question driving this. The medium changed. The instinct didn't.

Status

Books & Beds is currently in development. If you're curious about the project — as a traveler, a collaborator, or someone who just wants to follow along — reach out.
