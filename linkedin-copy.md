## ABOUT SECTION
*(First 2–3 lines appear before "see more" — make them count)*

I'm a product leader and strategist with a background that most people in this field don't have: I studied sociology before I built software. That training shaped how I think about products — not as features to ship, but as systems that change how people behave, work, and connect.

My career has moved between early-stage and large-scale, between public health and consumer tech, between the Global South and the US. That breadth is intentional. The hardest product problems tend to live at intersections — between sectors, between user groups, between what data shows and what it can't capture.

A few things I've worked on:

→ Led go-to-market strategy for Aarogya Setu, India's COVID contact-tracing app. 50 million users in 13 days. The work wasn't about technology — it was about trust infrastructure in a crisis.

→ Founded No Desk Project, a platform for remote and independent professionals. 8,000+ waitlist, 2,000+ community members, 6 countries, before I wound it down and joined Amazon.

→ At Amazon, I've worked on Alexa and Echo — thinking carefully about what meaningful engagement actually looks like for AI products, and how to measure what matters rather than what's easy to measure.

→ On the strategy side, I've helped companies navigate regulatory disruption, enter new markets, and identify where their product assumptions have quietly stopped being true.

I also teach. I was selected as one of 40 LEAD Facilitators at Chicago Booth, where I co-taught a full-credit leadership course to first-year MBA students. It's the work I find most personally demanding — and most clarifying.

I'm based in the US and I work across product strategy, go-to-market, and human-centered design. I'm particularly interested in products that reach people at scale without losing sight of the individual.

If you're building something that needs rigorous thinking at the intersection of product, people, and strategy — I'd like to hear about it.
