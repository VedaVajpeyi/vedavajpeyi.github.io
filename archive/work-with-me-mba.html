WORK WITH ME

MBA Positioning

The MBA application process often pushes applicants to flatten themselves into a committee-friendly version of who they are. This is a mistake — especially for people with non-linear paths.

Top programs are not looking for a perfect profile. They are looking for intellectual agency: how you think, how you make meaning of your experiences, and how you navigate ambiguity.

I work with a small number of applicants each year to identify the red thread in their background — the sociological and professional themes that actually define them — and turn that into a compelling, school-specific case for admission.

My Process

  • The Discovery Phase — A deep dive into your background before we touch an essay. We map patterns, motivations, inflection points, and choices — not just achievements.
  • The Human Draft — Moving past 'committee-speak' to write essays that sound like a specific, thoughtful, complicated human being.
  • The School Lens — Shaping your narrative for the distinct intellectual cultures of schools like Booth, Wharton, and Kellogg — without losing coherence or integrity.

This Is Best For

  • Non-Linear Paths — Founders, career-switchers, humanities majors, and candidates who feel that standard MBA advice doesn't quite apply to them.
  • The 'Whole Person' Applicant — People who want an application that feels rigorous and honest, not performative or overly polished.

How We'd Work Together

An initial diagnostic to understand your goals, constraints, and school strategy. A structured discovery process to surface the themes that actually define your story. Iterative narrative and essay work focused on clarity, coherence, and intellectual credibility.

I work with a limited number of applicants to ensure depth, not volume.

If you're looking for formulaic templates or résumé-to-essay translation, I'm likely not the right fit.
