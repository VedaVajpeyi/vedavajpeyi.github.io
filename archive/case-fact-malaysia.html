Turning Regulatory Disruption Into a $2M Opportunity

FACT Software · Malaysia · 2018

When Malaysia replaced GST with SST overnight, 100,000+ businesses suddenly needed new tax infrastructure. I identified the window before our competitors did, built the expansion strategy, and captured 20% regional market share in 6 months.

Situation

In 2018, the Malaysian government abolished the Goods and Services Tax (GST) and replaced it with the Sales and Services Tax (SST) — a fundamental shift in the country's tax architecture. The change was significant: over 100,000 businesses needed to update their accounting and ERP systems to comply with the new framework.

Most companies in our space were watching from the sidelines. The disruption was real, but the window was narrow — businesses would solve their tax compliance problem one way or another, quickly. The question was who would be there when they went looking.

At FACT Software, I was Head of Marketing with a mandate to grow market share in the Asia-Pacific region. When I saw the regulatory announcement, I recognized something most product and sales teams treat as noise: regulatory change is a buyer intent signal at scale.

My Role

Head of Marketing — responsible for identifying the opportunity, building the expansion strategy, and leading 7 cross-functional teams across product, sales, marketing, and regional operations to execute against it.

Approach

Identifying the Window

The first move was speed, not perfection. I convened an emergency cross-functional session within days of the announcement to size the opportunity: how many businesses were affected, what their compliance timelines looked like, and what our current product coverage was against SST requirements.

The answer was clear enough to move on: the market was large, the urgency was genuine, and our existing product could be adapted faster than a ground-up build would require. We didn't need to build new software. We needed to reposition and localize what we had.

Building the Strategy

I designed the expansion strategy around three pillars:

  • Product adaptation: Work with the engineering team to update FACT's tax modules for SST compliance, with documentation and release timed to the regulatory deadline.
  • Targeted GTM: Build sales collateral and messaging specifically for Malaysian SMBs navigating the transition — not generic ERP positioning, but language that met buyers where they actually were ("Your business needs to be SST-compliant by [date]. Here's how.").
  • Regional sales enablement: Train and equip the Malaysia-based sales team with objection handling, comparison guides, and a fast-path onboarding flow for compliance-motivated buyers who needed to move quickly.

Execution

I led 7 cross-functional teams through a compressed timeline, coordinating between product, engineering, regional sales, marketing, and legal. Speed was the primary constraint — businesses needed compliant software before the transition date, which meant our window had a hard close.

The go-to-market emphasized urgency without manufactured alarm: here is the deadline, here is what changes, here is how FACT solves it, here is how quickly you can get up and running.

Outcome

pipeline built within the first 3 months of the initiative

20%

7 teams

Compressed

timeline from opportunity identification to market execution

What I Learned

Regulatory disruption creates a category of buyers that almost never exists in normal markets: people who must buy, now, and have no loyalty to their previous vendor. The companies that win these windows are the ones who recognize the signal early and move before the opportunity becomes obvious to everyone else.

This experience also reinforced something I carry into every product and strategy conversation: customer problems don't always announce themselves as product problems. Sometimes they come dressed as compliance deadlines, regulatory changes, or operational crises. The job is to recognize them for what they are.
